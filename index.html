Experience

PM for Pixel @ Google

Aug 2019 - 2020

Pixel 4a: As the software PM for the Pixel 4a, I was responsible for the holistic software experience of the device and liaised between... well, everyone (software, hardware, product marketing, PR, GTM, customer support, and more).

I represented the device internally and externally — pre-briefing dozens of tech journalists across EMEA, presenting at Pixel-wide All-Hands, and communicating weekly updates to senior leadership. The role naturally gave me so many insights into releasing a physical device, as I was involved in everything from the product marketing narrative to crafting PR & customer support responses.

Pixel Essential Apps: I defined a product vision for the Bedtime experience in the Android Clock app, driving the project from concept to a successful launch (Bedtime now sees XXmil users each day). It was a complex effort spanning multiple internal teams and a couple of external partnerships (e.g. Calm, Spotify).

Head of Product & Design @ Archer

May - Sept 2018

Archer was a startup focusing on making public data accessible. We created Archer Public, a platform to visualize how companies and people are connected across borders, datasets, and documents — and make these insights verifiable and actionable to everyday citizens.

My role was spanned full-stack engineering, design, product strategy, and more. I built the data API and parts of the React frontend, as well as architected the underlying knowledge graph. Additionally, I worked on all of the design, including the platform's branding and UI/UX.

Project Lead @ Archer Labs

Aug 2017 - May 2018

I built SanctionsExplorer, the most modern (and most loved by the community!) publicly-available platform for searching and understanding U.S. Treasury sanctions. (Note: the current site's UI has changed since I worked on it; it used to look like this.)

I conducted user interviews, designed the platform's interface, and built the frontend and backend (all open-source!). I worked with two other Berkeley students on the cloud deployment and XML data parser.

We launched in April 2018 to an overwhelming amount of positive feedback from the international community, including multiple federal agencies, journalists & media organizations, academic researchers, NGOs, and private banks & companies around the world. The Treasury recognized our work, and now I'm the proud owner of a Challenge coin from the Treasury!

Software Engineering Intern @ Tanium

Jun - Aug 2017

As an intern, I developed a plug-and-play NodeJS module that could be integrated into many separate (but similar) products that Tanium offered. This module allowed developers to specify which metrics should be exposed to customers; these metrics would then be collected and could be displayed (which was more difficult than it sounds, given the arbitrarity of the data) to both technical and nontechnical customers.
