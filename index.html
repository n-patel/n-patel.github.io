Experience

Software Engineering Intern @ Tanium

Tanium is a large cybersecurity startup that provides a cutting-edge platform for endpoint management to many of the top Fortune 500 companies.

During the summer of 2017, I was hired as one of the interns for their first official intern program. I spent most of the summer tackling one large problem: the collection of diagnostics on a per-product level. Though the platform itself had some basic diagnostics, the products (which run their own separate Node servers) had no unified way to get diagnostics off of them.

I developed a standardized system to do so -- each team could import my Node module and specify which core functions they thought determined the health of the server/product. One of the core design principles of my module was to be very plug & play, requiring minimal changes to the existing codebase.

I also designed an intelligently rendered front-end, which would take the diagnostic data and attempt to render it in the most sensible way possible. This was an interesting design challenge, as I tried to support many layers of nesting (e.g. if a large JSON dictionary was returned, how do you view this in a way that's very readable?).

Software Consultant @ Archer International

Archer is a nonprofit focused on human-centered security and data-driven open-source investigations. We strive to "give researchers, journalists, grassroots organizations, and freelance investigators access to tools they need to protect people from war crimes, illicit finance of terrorism, violent misinformation, human trafficking, and more."

I've worked in a small team to develop a proof-of-concept for a new way to gather and store evidence for the International Criminal Court. Using IPFS, we created a prototype for offloading evidence in a decentralized, scalable, and secure way.

Undergraduate Research Assistant @ UC Berkeley

Julia is a new high-level yet high-performance programming language used for numerical computing. It gives the flexibility and readability of languages like Python while providing speeds comparable to C.

I work closely with Professor David Anthoff to implement and optimize packages that are provide additional data science abstractions in Julia. Currently, we're working on optimizing the performance of Query.jl (inspired by C#'s LINQ).

Software Consultant @ Blockchain at Berkeley

Our small team worked to develop a proof-of-concept decentralized web service for FDA-compliant supply-chain management for pharmaceutical companies. The Drug Supply Chain Security Act mandates that, over the next few years, pharmaceutical companies have more rigorous insights into their supply chain in order to help identify counterfeit, contaminated, or dangerous drugs -- and to remove these drugs before they're sold to consumers.

We wrote a smart contract that runs on the Ethereum blockchain to accomplish this, and presented it to the organization and members of the blockchain industry at the end of the semester (after dozens of hours of research, emails, coding, and debugging). We were awarded with the Best Project distinction.
